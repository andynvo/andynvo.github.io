Introduction to Endocrine Physiology

Chemical Classification of Hormones

  1. Peptide hormone
    • CRH, TRH, GnRH, GHRH, somatostatin, ACTH, TSH, LH, FHS, GH, prolactin, ADH, oxytocin, PTH, insulin, glucagon, IGF-1
  2. Amino acid-derived hormone
    • dopamine, epinephrine, norepinephrine, thyroxine (T4), triiodothyroxine (T3)
  3. Steroid hormone
    • cortisol, aldosterone, estrogens, androgens, progesterone

Hormone-binding proteins

  • Thyroxine (T4) - thyroid binding globulin, TPBA, albumin
  • Cortisol - cortisol binding globulin, albumin
  • Testosterone - sex hormone binding globulin (SHBG)

Insulin

Insulin synthesis and secretion (source: UWorld)

Biosynthesis

Insulin is a small protein composed of two polypeptide chains, A and B, join by a disulfide linkage. It is synthesized as a preprohormone, requiring a series of proteolytic cleavage in the endoplasmaic reticulum and Golgi complex to make the active form of insulin (51 amino acids) and an inactive C-peptide (31 amino acids). The C-peptide is deriverd by the cleavage of proinsulin to insulin. On stimulation of the β‑cells, insulin and C-peptide are released into circulation (in equimolar concentration).

Extra
[]
  • In patients with hypoglycemia 2° to high circulating insulin, C-peptide levels help distinguish between endogenous (e.g., insulinoma) and exogenous (e.g., surreptitious use) sources.
  • C-peptide levels can also be used to asses remainign endogeonous insulin production in patients receiving exogenous insullin (e.g., DM Ⅱ).

Secretion

Insulin secretion (source: First Aid for USMLE Step 1, p.334)

Glucose enters β‑cells via the GLUT 2 transporter via faciliated diffusion. Increases in serum glucose within β‑ cells are shunted into glycolytic pathwasy. Increased glucose catabolism leads to a rise in intracellular ATP:ADP ratio, which causes the ATP‑sensitive K+ channel to close. Colsure of this channel leads to depolarization and opening of voltage‑gated Ca2+ channels. Rise in intracellular Ca2+ causes release of insulin-containing vesicles.

Another mediator of insulin release in the 2° messenger cAMP. Serum glucose stimiulates cAMP formation within β‑cell, which mobilizes intracellular calcium stores and stimulate insulin granule exocytosis.
