Hypertension therapies

Angiotensin‑converting Enzyme Inhibitors

captopril, enlapril, lisinopril, ramipril
MECHANISM OF ACTION ⊣ ACE → ↓ ATⅡ → ↓ GFR by preventing constriction of efferent arterioles
↳ ↑ renin due to loss of negative feedback
↳ prevents inactivation of bardykinin, a potent vasodialtor
CLINICAL USE HTN, HF (↓ mortality)
↳ prevent unfavorable heart remodeling from chronic HTN
Proteinuria, diabetic nephropathy
ADVERSE EFFECTS Heavier or longer menses, dysmenorrhea. Risk of PID with insertion
CONTRAINDICATIONS CATCHH:
  • Cough,
  • Angioedema,
  • Teratogen,
  • ↑ creatinine,
  • Hyperkalemia
  • Hypotension

Angiotensin Ⅱ Receptor Blockers (ARBs)

losartan, candesartan, valsartan
MECHANISM OF ACTION Selectively block binding of angiotensin Ⅱ to AT1 receptor
↳ similar effects to ACEi, but ARBs don't ↑ bardykinin
CLINICAL USE HTN, HF, proteinuria, chronic kidney disease with intolerance to ACEi
ADVERSE EFFECTS Heavier or longer menses, dysmenorrhea. Risk of PID with insertion
CONTRAINDICATIONS Hyperkalemia, ↓ GFR, hypotension, teratogen
