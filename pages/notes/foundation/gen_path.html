Growth Adaptations

In general, an organ is in homeostasis with the normal physiologic stress placed upon it. When there is a change in this stress (whether ↑ or ↓), growth adaptation occurs.

  1. Hypertrophy refers to the increase in cell size, by gene activation, protein synthesis, and production of organelles.
  2. Hyperplasia is the increase in cell number, by production of new cells from stem cells.
  3. Atrophy refers to the decrease in cell size (via ubiquitin-proteosome degration of cytoskeleton and autophagy of cellular components) and cell number (by apoptosis).
  4. Metaplasia is the change in cell type. For example, a change from one type of surface epithelium (squamous, columnar, or urothelial) to another.
  5. Dysplasia is disordered cell growth and most often refers to precancerous growth.
  6. Aplasia is failure of cell production during embryogenesis.
  7. Hypoplasia is decrease in cell production during embryogenesis.
