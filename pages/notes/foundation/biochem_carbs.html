Cell Signaling

Types of Signaling

  • Endocrine: signaling molecules are released into the nearby blood vessels and travel all over the body to reach its target organ(s), tissue(s), or cells
  • Paracrine: signaling molecules are released into the cell's surrounding environment and affect local cells
  • Autocrine: signaling molecules are released into the cell's immediate environment and affect itself

General Model of Signaling Pathway

A cell released a signaling molecule, and this molecule travels until it reaches its destination, determined by the type of signaling as well as the location of the target receptors.
