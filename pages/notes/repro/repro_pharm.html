Contraception

Intrauterine Devices

Copper-releasing IUD

MECHANISM OF ACTION Produces local inflammatory reaction toxic to sperm and ova, preventing fertilization and implantation
↳ reduce sperm function and motility
↳ disrupt normal division of oocytes and formation of fertilizable ovarian
↳ disrupt endometrium sufficiently to prevent implantation
CLINICAL USE Long-acting reversible contraception. Most effective emergency contraception (up to 120 hrs/5 days following unprotected intercourse).
↳ failure rate: 0.7% in 1 year, 2.2% in 12 years
↳ lasts 10+ years
ADVERSE EFFECTS Heavier or longer menses, dysmenorrhea. Risk of PID with insertion
CONTRAINDICATIONS Active pelvic infection, pregnancy or suspected pregnancy, distorted uterine cavity, undx'd uterine bleeding, malignancy of genital tract, allergy to component (or Wilson's dz)

Levonorgestrel IUD (also hLARC)

Brand names: Mirena, Liletta, Kyleena
MECHANISM OF ACTION Releases 5-20 mcg levonorgestrel per day, primarily local effects
↳ sperm motility and function impaired
↳ cervical mucus thickened
↳ endometrium maintained in "resting" state
↳ ovulation inhibited only in some cycles
No estrogen
CLINICAL USE Long-acting reversible contraception.
↳ failure rate: 0.1-0.8% per year
↳ lasts 5-6 years depending on brand Endometreial production for women receiving estrogen replacement (OFF-LABEL)
ADVERSE EFFECTS Heavy menses
CONTRAINDICATIONS Active pelvic infection, pregnancy or suspected pregnancy, distorted uterine cavity, undx'd uterine bleeding, malignancy of genital tract

Hormonal Long-acting Reversible Contraceptives (LARCs)

Etonogestrel implant

Brand names: Nexplanon
MECHANISM OF ACTION Releases 20-60 mcg etonogestrel per day; suppresses ovluation, thicken cervical mucus, alters endometrium
No estrogen
CLINICAL USE Long-acting reversible contraception.
↳ lasts for 3 years
ADVERSE EFFECTS Menstrual irregularities
CONTRAINDICATIONS same as other progestin-only methods*

Combined Hormonal Contraceptives

MECHANISM OF ACTION Progestins + ethinl estradiol; forms include pill, patch (Xulane), and intravaginal ring (NuvaRing)
↳ both inhibit LH/FSH and thus prevent estrogen surge. No estrogen surge → no LH surge → no ovulation.
↳ progestins causes thickening of mucus, thereby lmiting access of sperm to uterus.
↳ progesting also inhibit endometrial proliferation → less suitable for implantation
CLINICAL USE Long-acting reversible contraception.
ADVERSE EFFECTS breakthrough menstrual bleeding, breast tenderness, VTE, hepatic adenomas
CONTRAINDICATIONS smokers > 35 y/o, pts with ↑ risk of CV dz, migrane (esp. w/ aura), breast cancer, liver cancer

Progestin-only Pills

Generics: levonorgestrel, medroxyprogesterone, etonogestrel, norethinodrone, megestrol
MECHANISM OF ACTION Binds progesterone receptors, ↓ growth and ↑ vascularization of endometrium, thicken cervical mucus
CLINICAL USE Contraception (including pills, IUDs, implants [Nexplanon], and depot injection [DMPA])
endometreial cancer, abnormal uterine bleeding
ADVERSE EFFECTS hypoesterogenism, breast tenderness, ↑ appeptite & weight; acne (only w/o estrogen)
DMPA - loss of bone mineral density, teratogenesis
CONTRAINDICATIONS Current breast cancer, known or suspected pregnancy, VTE, liver tumors, active liver dz, undiagnosed abnormal genital bleeding, known sensitivity to progestin
