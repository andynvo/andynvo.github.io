Renal Pharmacology

In this section, we will be discussing diuretics, its mechanism of action (MOA); the clinical indications, contraindications, and considerations; and side effects.

Basic Pharmacology

Osmotic diuretic

Generic: Mannitol
Brand names:
MECHANISM OF ACTION ↑ tubular fluid osmolarity ⇒ ↑ urine flow, ↓ ICP/IOP
INDICATION drug O/D, ↑ ICP/IOP
CONTRAINDICATION anuria, HF
ADVERSE EFFECTS Pulmonary edema, dehydration, hypo- or hypernatremia
NOTES Fast-acting method of reducing ICP

Carbonic anhydrase inhibitor

Generic: Acetazolamide
Brand names:
MECHANISM OF ACTION Causes self-limited sodium bicarb, diuresis, and decrease body bicar stores.
Alkalinizes urine
INDICATION Glaucoma, metabolic alkalosis, altitude sickness, idiopathic intracranial HTN
CONTRAINDICATION sulfa allergy
ADVERSE EFFECTS Proximal renal tubular acidosis, parethesias, urea toxicity, hypokalemia
Promotes calcium phosphate stone formation
NOTES

Loop diuretics

Generic: furosemide, bumetanide, torsemide (sulfonamide); ethacrynic acid (non-sulfa)
Brand names: Lasix
MECHANISM OF ACTION inhibits NKCC in TAL
prevents concentration of urine
↑ Ca2+ excretion
INDICATION edematous states (HF, nephrotic syndrome, pulmonary edema)
HTN, hypercalcemia
CONTRAINDICATION sulfa allergy
ADVERSE EFFECTS ototoxicity, hypokalemia, hypomagnesemia, dehydration, metabolic alkalosis, interstitial nephritis, gout
NOTES

Thiazide diuretics

Generic: hydrochlorothiazide (HCTZ), chlorthalidone, metolazone
Brand names:
MECHANISM OF ACTION Inhibits NaCl co-transporter in DCT
↓ Ca2+ excretion
INDICATION HTN, HF, idiopathic hypercalciuria, nephrogenic diabetes insipidus, osteoporosis
CONTRAINDICATION sulfa allergy
ADVERSE EFFECTS hypokalemic metabolic alkalosis, hyponatremia, hyperglycemia, hyperlipidemia, hyperuricemia, hypercalcemia
NOTES

Potassium-sparing diuretics

Generic: spironolactone, eplerenone, amiloride, triamterine
Brand names:
MECHANISM OF ACTION -one: competitive aldosterone receptor antagonist
other 2: block ENaC
INDICATION hyperaldosteronism, K+ depletion, HF, hepatic ascites (spironolactone), nephrogenic DI (amiloride), antiandrogen
CONTRAINDICATION
ADVERSE EFFECTS hyperkalemia (can lead to arrhythmias), endocrine effect with spironolactone (eg, gynocomastia, antiandrogen fx)
NOTES

Aliskiren

Brand names:
MECHANISM OF ACTION direct renin inhibitor
INDICATION HTN
CONTRAINDICATION ACEi or ARBs usage (relative)
pregnancy (absolute)
ADVERSE EFFECTS hyperkalemia, decrease GFR, hypotension, angioedema
NOTES
